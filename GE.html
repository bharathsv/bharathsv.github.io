Block-Based Conditional Entropy Coding for Medical Image Compression [pdf]
Proc. of SPIE Medical Imaging, February 2003, San Diego, USA.

Invented block-based conditional entropy coding technique for medical image compression to achieve compression ratios better than JPEG-2000. Instead of using same scan order (in the computation of conditional entropy) on the wavelet coefficients in different bands, different scan orders are investigated and a sub-optimal scan order with optimal block size is determined for the given data set to perform compression.

This project was carried out as a part of proprietary image codec development, which was presented as TruRez: A Fast Wavelet-Based Multi-resolution Image Compression Scheme for Medical Images in InfoRAD presentation session at RSNA, November 2002, Chicago, USA.


Three-Dimensional Integer Wavelet Transforms for Medical Image Compression [pdf]
Proc. of IEEE ICIP, October 2004, Singapore.

Investigated different multi-resolution integer wavelets, combination of integer wavelets and prediction transforms to achieve higher compression ratios with low decoding complexity.

Developed a novel progressive thick slab paradigm for 3-D volume compression with features such as to reduce waiting time for medical image transmission across low-bandwidth networks, low decoding complexity, higher compression ratios and efficient navigation. Developed a clinical workflow for efficient navigation of large medical data sets.

This work was presented as 3-D Loss-less Multi-resolution Image Compression for Medical Images in InfoRAD presentation session at RSNA, November 2003, Chicago, USA. Based on this work, a US patent application titled Progressive Medical Image Volume Navigation is filed.


Texture Analysis for Automatic Metal-Loss Detection in Magnetic Flux Leakage (MFL) Seamless Pipes [pdf]
Proc. of SPCOM, December 2004, Bangalore, India.

Investigated texture based approach for automatic metal-loss detection in MFL (magnetic flux leakage) seamless pipes for automatic pipeline inspection.

Statistical-based, model-based and transform-based textural features are investigated to determine a feature list for efficient classification of metal-loss regions from background. The texture-based approach showed promise over other methods explained in the literature. Support Vector Machine based classifier is implemented and is used for classification.


Acoustic Tomography

Investigated the acoustic tomographic reconstruction approach (iterative algebraic reconstruction techniques) for acoustic pyrometry. The idea is to use tomographic reconstruction techniques to reconstruct the temperature profiles along the cross-section of a gas exhaust using acoustic transducers.


Shape Matching

The shape changes in the hippocampus from MR images of Alzheimer's and control patients are studied by deforming the hippocampus of a given set of subjects to a reference and computing the amount of deformation involved for Alzheimer's and normal patients using thin-plate splines. This amount of shape change is studied as a marker for early diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease.

This work is presented as Spatial Distribution of T2 values in the Hippocampus of Alzheimer's Disease and Control Subjects at ISMRM, May 2004, Kyoto, Japan.


Image-Based Markers for Early Diagnosis of Alzheimer's Disease[pdf]
Proc. of SPIE Medical Imaging, February 2005, San Diego, USA.

It is well understood that the iron content in the hippocampus is an indicator of Alzheimer's Disease. T2 data from MRI captures the iron content and so features from T2 can be extracted to be used as markers for Alzheimer's detection.

I studied the second order statistics of T2 which in turn capture the textural content in T2 and it provided high sensitivities (better than shape based features) on a logistic regression classifier providing an interesting and useful marker for Alzheimer's disease.

Multi-modality Medical Image Registration [pdf]
Proc. of Applied Imagery and Pattern Recognition, October 2005, Washington DC, USA.

In certain medical image modalities, affine registration is not sufficient for good registration while at the same time non-rigid registration is computationally intensive. Also, non-rigid registration methods are studied mainly on mono-modality, intra-patient data sets. So, we need a method which is non-linear but can be used on multi-modality cases and is not computationally intensive.

We proposed a piece-wise affine transformation which provides piece-wise linear approximation to non-rigid transformation and is computationally cheap compared to non-rigid registration. Mutual-information (MI) is used as the registration metric and it performs well on multi-modality cases.
